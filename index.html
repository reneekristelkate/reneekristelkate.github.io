About

I hold a Bachelor of Science in Business Administration degree with a specialization in Business Analytics, which I earned from Silliman University in Dumaguete. My academic journey initially began with a different goal, as I originally pursued a path toward becoming an accountant. However, after two semesters of enlightenment, I discovered that my true passion lay elsewhere, leading me to embrace the dynamic and data-driven field of Business Analytics.

Why Business Analytics?

Recognizing the evolving landscape of our data-driven world, I have always held the conviction that a career in analytics holds tremendous promise – a belief that remains true. In a global arena marked by intensifying competition, the abundant availability of data has become both a challenge and an opportunity for businesses. Analysts play a pivotal role in this scenario, tasked with the responsibility of crafting more effective strategies to harness data's potential for informed decision-making and optimization.

My internship experience, which involved handling vast datasets, has strengthened my belief in the essential role of analytics. As the sheer volume of available data continues to grow, so do the complexities surrounding its management. Questions arise regarding data accuracy, consistency, and relevance, raising concerns about data quality and how to effectively harness this wealth of information. It is in addressing these pivotal questions that the training I received proves invaluable.


Let's go back further in time.

I spent my entire high school tenure at Saint Louis School of Don Bosco, Inc. in the same city. During this period, I actively participated in a variety of competitions both within and outside the school. Notable events included the Metrobank-MTAP-DepEd Math Challenge, Division, Regional, and National Schools Press Conference, as well as competitive chess tournaments. I also held leadership positions as the editor-in-chief of the school publication and the yearbook. Despite my involvement in these activities, I eventually realized that none of them truly resonated with my passion. Nevertheless, I continued to uphold the value instilled in me during my formative years– commitment to excellence. This commitment remained a guiding principle throughout my college journey. I achieved recognition on the Dean's List twice and consistently qualified for the Honor Scholarship Grant. Additionally, I was honored as a Class Honor and College Honor for multiple years. While I may not have been as active in extracurricular activities during this period, I did serve as a member of the College of Business Administration's COMELEC and as an Auditor for the Business Analytics Students' Society during the 2020-2021 academic year. I then finally graduated with a TQPA of 3.66.
